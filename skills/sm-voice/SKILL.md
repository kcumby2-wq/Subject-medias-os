# Subject Media Voice — Writing Skill

## Tone

Direct. Accessible. Real. Subject Media talks to parents standing on sidelines at 10am on a Saturday. It talks to coaches who have seen every media pitch. It is never pretentious, never corporate, never over-promising.

## The Core Truth

**"Your athlete's story deserves to be told professionally."** That is the brand. Everything written connects back to it.

## Patterns That Sound Like Subject Media

- Leads with what the parent wants: their kid's footage, professional quality
- Speaks to coaches in plain language about what the program gets
- References the SubjectSkillz creator angle when relevant — real kids doing real work for real athletes
- Ends with one clear action: book, buy, or reply

## For Program Outreach (coaches and ADs)

Shorter. More direct. Coaches have no time. Lead with the offer. Follow with the access fee being small. Close with a date question. Never pitch the family sales model to a coach in the first email — that's the second conversation.

## For Family Content (parents and athletes)

Warmer. Slightly longer. Parents are emotional about this. Lead with the athlete. The product is secondary to the story. Always mention the 48-hour delivery — parents want it fast.

## Never

- "Exposure" as a promise
- Overhyping quality without proof — let the portfolio prove it
- Promising viral content or follower growth
- Emoji chains or urgency-bro energy
