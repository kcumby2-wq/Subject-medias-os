### Cold Email Sequence — Individual Programs

**Email 1 — Day 1 — The Offer**
Subject: Professional media for [TEAM NAME] — at no real cost to your program
Body: "Coach [Name] — I run Subject Media, a professional sports media company that covers club programs across the country. We want to cover [TEAM NAME] at your next event. Your program pays a small access fee — we sell coverage packages directly to your families. Your athletes get professional footage. Your program pays almost nothing. Can I send you details?" — Three sentences. One ask.

**Email 2 — Day 4 — The Numbers**
Subject: What a game day looks like for [TEAM NAME]
Body: Send the revenue picture from the Teams pitch doc. Show them a varsity program at $349 access fee generating $4,000+ in family sales. Frame it as proof the model works — not a sales pitch.

**Email 3 — Day 8 — The Close**
Subject: One question for [TEAM NAME]
Body: "When is your next tournament or event? I want to get you on the schedule before the season fills up." Direct. One question. Forces a response.

**Email 4 — Day 14 — Final Follow**
Subject: Last note — [TEAM NAME]
Body: "Didn't want to keep filling your inbox. If you want professional coverage for your athletes this season just reply and I'll get you set up. If the timing isn't right I'll reach back out next season." Removes pressure. Gets replies from people who were interested but busy.

### Cold Call Script — Individual Programs

**Opening:** "Hey Coach [Name], this is Kyron from Subject Media. I work with club programs across the country — I used to work with Pylon so I know the circuit well. I want to cover your program this season. Quick question — when's your next tournament?"

**If they engage:** "Here's how it works. You pay a small access fee to have us on site — anywhere from $199 to $499 depending on your program size. We sell coverage packages directly to your families at the event. They pay us, not you. Your athletes get professional footage and you look like you invested in your kids. That's it."

**The close:** "What's your next event date? I'll get you on the calendar."

### Tournament Organization Pitch — Separate Script

**Opening:** "Hey [Name], this is Kyron from Subject Media. I've worked Pylon events before so I know how these tournaments run. I want to bring our media team to your next event — not for one team, for all of them. I want to be your official media partner."

**The pitch:** "You charge your teams to be in the tournament. We charge you one flat fee to be the official media provider for the event. Then every family in the building can buy coverage packages for their athlete directly from us. You make money on the partnership. We make money on the families. Every athlete at your event goes home with professional footage."

**The close:** "What's your next event? Let me send you the partnership terms."

### GHL Outreach Sequence Setup

Load all 1,500 contacts into GHL segmented by sport tag. Build four-email sequence per sport with sport-specific language — a 7v7 parent reads differently than a club volleyball parent. Set sequence to start automatically when contact is tagged. Monitor open rates weekly. Any contact that opens email 2 but doesn't respond gets a manual call from Kyron.

## Component 3 — On-Site Operations

### Creator Roles on Site

| Role | Responsibility | Equipment |
|---|---|---|
| Lead Filmer | Wide angle · evaluation angles · coaching moments | Sony FX30 or equivalent |
| Support Filmer | Close-up · athlete-specific · sideline | Sony a7III or equivalent |
| Photographer | Action · portrait · sideline · family moments | DSLR · 70-200mm lens |
| On-Site Coordinator | Family sales · QR codes · consent forms · footage logging | Phone + tablet |

### The QR Code System

Every event gets a unique QR code generated in GHL. Code links to a landing page pre-loaded with the team or tournament name. Family scans the code, selects their package, pays through Stripe, and enters the GHL pipeline automatically. No manual order taking. No cash. No confusion.

QR codes printed on: small cards handed to families at check-in · posted at the sideline · sent via text to team contacts before the event.

### Consent Collection on Site

Simple one-page consent form — paper or digital via QR code. Required before any athlete's individual footage is sold or posted publicly. Collected at the same moment families purchase packages. On-site coordinator manages this.

Tournament events: consent collected at team check-in through the tournament coordinator.
Program events: coach distributes consent forms to families at arrival.

### Footage Logging System

Every clip logged at the event with: athlete name (if known), team, sport, drill or game type, camera operator. Simple Google Sheet or AirTable on the coordinator's tablet. Synced to the creator catalog within 24 hours of the event.

Logged footage goes into three buckets: sold (family purchased) · catalog (available for creator assignments) · archive (stored but not active).

### Event Day Checklist

**Before arriving:** QR codes printed · consent forms ready · GHL event contact list loaded · Stripe products active · creator assignments confirmed.

**On arrival:** Scout filming locations · introduce Subject Media to program or tournament coordinator · set up coordinator station · brief creators on athlete priority list.

**During event:** Film continuously · coordinator works the sideline selling packages · consent forms collected at point of sale · footage logged in real time.

**After event:** All footage transferred and logged within 2 hours · GHL follow-up sequence fires automatically · editing assignments sent to creators · content delivered to families within 48 hours.

## Component 4 — Revenue Flow

### How Money Moves

1. Organization or program pays access fee → Stripe invoice → GHL tags them as active partner.
2. Family purchases package at event via QR code → Stripe → GHL contact created automatically → tagged by package type and event.
3. TOJ collects 100% of all revenue through Stripe. Creator compensation calculated per event and paid through SubjectSkillz.
4. If A1 is involved in the GHL build for Subject Media events → 65/35 split on that specific work per the partnership agreement.

### Creator Compensation Per Event

| Creator Role | Per Event Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lead Filmer | $75–150 | Based on event size and hours |
| Support Filmer | $50–100 | Based on event size and hours |
| Photographer | $50–100 | Based on event size and hours |
| On-Site Coordinator | $50–75 | Flat rate per event |
| Video Editor | $15–30 per clip | Paid per delivered package |
| Graphic Designer | $10–20 per graphic | Paid per delivered graphic |

All creator compensation runs through SubjectSkillz 501(c)(3). Rates are intentionally modest — this is a portfolio-building program. Rates increase at Level 3 as creators demonstrate consistent quality.

### Monthly Revenue Projections

| Scale | Events/Month | Avg Revenue/Event | Monthly Total | Annual Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starting Out | 4 events | $2,500 | $10,000 | $120,000 |
| Growing | 8 events | $3,500 | $28,000 | $336,000 |
| At Scale | 16 events | $4,500 | $72,000 | $864,000 |
| Tournament Partner | 4 majors | $8,000 | $32,000 | $384,000 |

Revenue per event = organization/program access fee + family package sales. Conservative family participation assumed at 25–40% of athletes per event.

### The Tournament Channel Upside

A major tournament with 32 teams and 25 athletes per team = 800 athletes in one building over one weekend. If 30% of families buy the $99 Video Coverage package that is $23,760 from one weekend event. Plus the $1,999 organization fee. One tournament relationship at scale generates more revenue than 10 individual program relationships.

## Component 5 — GHL Pipeline

### The Three Contact Types in GHL

| Contact Type | How They Enter | Primary Tag | Pipeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Program / Coach | Cold outreach or inbound inquiry | sm-program | Program Pipeline |
| Tournament Org | Direct outreach or referral | sm-tournament | Tournament Pipeline |
| Family / Athlete | QR code purchase at event | sm-family | Family Pipeline |

### Program Pipeline — Stages

Contacted → Responded → Agreement Sent → Access Fee Paid → Event Scheduled → Event Complete → Season Partner (upsell)

### Tournament Pipeline — Stages

Identified → Outreach Sent → Call Scheduled → Proposal Sent → Agreement Signed → Event Confirmed → Post-Event Review → Renewal

### Family Pipeline — Stages

Package Purchased → Content In Production → Delivered → SR Upsell Sent → Converted or Archived

### Key Automations

Family purchases package → instant confirmation SMS + email → creator assignment created → 48hr delivery reminder → delivery confirmed → SR $249 upsell fires automatically.

Program pays access fee → event confirmed SMS → pre-event logistics email 48hrs before → post-event thank you + season partner offer → 30-day re-engagement.

Tournament org signs agreement → event briefing document sent → coordinator assigned → post-event recap + renewal conversation triggered.

### Sport-Specific Tags

Every contact tagged by sport on entry: `sm-7v7` · `sm-aau-basketball` · `sm-club-volleyball` · `sm-club-soccer` · `sm-lacrosse`. Enables sport-specific broadcast campaigns — when Subject Media is covering a 7v7 tournament, only 7v7 contacts in that region get the notification.

### The SR Upsell Sequence

Every family who purchases a media package enters a 3-email SR upsell sequence starting 48 hours after content delivery.

**Email 1:** "Your athlete's footage is professionally covered. Now make sure coaches know how to evaluate what they're seeing. Get your verified SubjectReport transcript for $249."

**Email 2 (Day 5):** "Coaches don't watch highlight tapes. They evaluate film. Here's the difference — and here's how to get a transcript that coaches actually use."

**Email 3 (Day 10):** "Last note — if your athlete is serious about recruiting this season, the transcript is the next step. Link below."
